Why Multispectral

Healthy vegetation strongly reflects near-infrared (NIR) light—revealing stress and patterns invisible to RGB photos. Eagle60 captures calibrated NIR and produces color-infrared (CIR) imagery to help you measure plant vigor, classify surfaces, and monitor change.

  • Vegetation health: detect stress, disease, and nutrient deficiencies early.
  • Surface classification: separate vegetation, soil, water, and impervious surfaces.
  • Risk assessment: evaluate fuels and conditions relevant to wildfire spread.

What Are NIR & CIR?

NIR: light outside human vision that living plants reflect from internal cell structure—ideal for assessing vigor.

CIR: a “false-color” composite mapping NIR→red, red→green, and green→blue, making healthy vegetation appear bright red.

How It’s Captured

1) Multispectral Sensors

Professional payloads capture discrete bands (e.g., red, green, blue, NIR) with photogrammetry-grade calibration.

2) Processing

We process bands into aligned products (NIR, CIR) and derived layers for analysis and mapping.

3) Repeatable Missions

Logs and flight specs allow precise re-flights for time series and before/after comparisons.
