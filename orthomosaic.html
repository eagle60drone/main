What is an Orthomosaic?

An orthomosaic is a map-accurate image created by stitching dozens to thousands of overlapping aerial photos that have been geometrically corrected (orthorectified) to remove distortion and perspective. The result: a seamless, scale-correct top-down view.

How It’s Made

1) Flight Planning

Mission plans ensure full coverage with precise overlap and ground control as required.

2) Image Capture

High-resolution, overlapping photos captured on a programmed flight path for consistent results.

3) Orthorectify & Stitch

Specialized software corrects geometry and stitches orthophotos into a seamless, map-accurate mosaic.
